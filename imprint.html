The Problem

Every AI has an amnesia tax. It forgets you the moment the session ends. So you repeat yourself, re-explain your context, and settle for generic answers from a tool that should know you by now.

What Imprint Does

Your AI gets smarter about you — because it reads your data, not everyone's.

Imprint creates an evolving profile of how you operate — your processing style, communication preferences, working patterns, values, and goals. This profile lives in your personal vault, and AI reads it before every conversation.

It remembers.

Not through chat history that gets truncated, and not through platform memory that belongs to the AI company. Imprint stores structured observations about you in your personal vault — portable, inspectable, and yours.

Your operator profile captures things like how you process information, what drains your energy, what triggers your best work, and how you want AI to interact with you.

Operator profile preview

It challenges.

Standard AI is designed to be agreeable. Imprint is designed to be useful. It pressure-tests your premises, finds the gaps in your logic, and tells you when an idea isn't ready — before the real world does.

This isn't generic pushback. It's calibrated to your patterns — Imprint knows where you tend to over-analyze, where you move too fast, and where your blind spots live.

Conversation example

It grows with you.

Imprint doesn't just remember a static profile. It accumulates observations over time — patterns it notices, hypotheses about how you work, changes it detects. Every session adds to the picture.

You can review, correct, or delete any observation. The AI proposes what it notices. You decide what's true.

How Imprint Thinks

Four commitments.

🧠

"I don't forget."

A conversation isn't a series of isolated prompts — it's a relationship. Imprint reads your history, your context, your goals, and your patterns before it says a word. It knows you better today than it did yesterday.

⚡

"I don't flatter."

Validation is a metric for social networks, not for personal growth. Imprint strips the noise — the hedging, the praise, the filler — to maximize signal density. If an idea is good, it's because the logic checks out.

🛡

"I protect your energy."

The most valuable thing an AI can do is say no. Imprint monitors your bandwidth and protects your attention. It flags when you're overextending, and it won't let you burn cycles on low-value work.

🔑

"Your data, always."

Everything Imprint knows about you lives in your personal vault. Not in a company database. Not in a platform's memory store. In a place you own, can inspect, and can take with you if you leave.

How It's Different

Not another chatbot with memory bolted on.

Platform AI memory

  • The AI company owns and stores your memory
  • You can't inspect or export what it "remembers"
  • Memory is platform-locked — switch tools, lose everything
  • Same generic persona for every user
  • Optimized for engagement, not for you

Imprint

  • Your profile lives in your personal vault
  • Every observation is visible, editable, deletable
  • Your context is portable — take it anywhere
  • Calibrated to your specific patterns and preferences
  • Optimized for your growth and your goals
